<p><p>The blood sensor market is categorized into minimally invasive and non-invasive types. Minimally invasive sensors require small punctures or surface contact, providing accurate readings while ensuring patient comfort. Examples include devices that use microneedles or lancets for blood sampling. In contrast, non-invasive sensors analyze biometrics without skin penetration, employing technologies like spectroscopy or optical sensors. These solutions offer convenience and ease of use, capturing vital data from a distance, appealing to users seeking quick and painless health monitoring.</p></p>
