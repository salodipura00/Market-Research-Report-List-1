<p><p>The Extra Virgin Camellia Oil market features two primary extraction methods: expelling and lixiviation. The expelling process involves mechanically pressing the seeds to extract oil, preserving its natural flavor and nutrients. This method is often preferred for its purity. In contrast, the lixiviation process uses solvents to dissolve the oil from the seeds, resulting in a higher yield but potentially compromising quality. Both methods cater to different consumer demands, impacting market pricing and availability in the culinary and cosmetic sectors.</p></p>
