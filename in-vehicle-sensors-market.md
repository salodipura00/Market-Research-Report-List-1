<p><p>In the in-vehicle sensors market, two primary types exist: wired and wireless sensors. Wired sensors connect directly to the vehicle's electrical system, providing reliable data transmission and power supply, often used for critical functions like engine management and safety systems. In contrast, wireless sensors utilize radio frequency or other wireless technologies, offering flexibility in installation and ease of integration, particularly for applications like tire pressure monitoring and environmental sensing. Both types play vital roles in enhancing vehicle performance and safety.</p></p>
